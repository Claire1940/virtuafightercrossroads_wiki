<Section icon={Shield} title="Defensive Layers and the Combat Triangle">

Defending in **virtua fighter crossroads** requires more than just holding a button. The **Flow Guard** system introduces an active defensive layer that rewards timing and positioning. Unlike a standard block, Flow Guard allows a player to move fluidly while mitigating damage, setting up immediate counter-attack opportunities.

Despite these new additions, the core of the game remains the "Strike-Throw-Guard" triangle. This fundamental logic ensures that every action has a direct counter, maintaining the series' reputation for being a "fighting chess match."

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Triangle">
    Understand that **Strikes** beat throw attempts, **Guards** nullify strikes, and **Throws** punish passive guarding. This is the foundation of every 1v1 encounter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Implement Flow Guard">
    Practice activating Flow Guard during opponent pressure to maintain mobility. This is essential for navigating multi-opponent brawls in story mode.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Recognize Stance Shifts">
    High, Mid, and Low attacks require different guard heights. Standing Guard blocks High/Mid but loses to Lows and Throws.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Throw Escapes">
    When grabbed, inputting the correct direction plus the Punch+Guard button will break the throw, resetting the neutral game.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Defense Warning">
Over-reliance on Standing Guard makes you a prime target for the **Bakunawa Killer's** unique command throws. Mix up your defensive options to keep the opponent guessing.
</Callout>
