<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Battle Rules">

Virtua Fighter Crossroads retains the series' legendary close-range tactical depth while introducing modern mechanics that reshape the flow of battle. Fights are built on the classic strike-throw-guard triangle: strikes defeat throw attempts, guards block strikes, and throws punish passive guarding. On top of this foundation, the game introduces distinct rulesets and defensive systems that players must master to survive the scenario battles.

The combat system balances traditional round-based match play with dynamic, momentum-based mechanics. Review the core gameplay systems to understand how they alter the flow of combat:

| System | Category | How It Works | Player Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Rules** | Battle Rule | Traditional round-based matches decided by health depletion or ring outs. | Classic tactical play |
| **Uprising Rules** | Battle Rule | A dynamic ruleset built around shifting momentum and scoring conditions. | Fast-paced, dramatic matches |
| **Break & Rush** | Offensive System | Target specific body regions to accumulate permanent damage across rounds. | Long-term match strategy |
| **Flow Guard** | Defensive System | Active, visually fluid defensive maneuvers that counter aggressive pressure. | High-level counterplay |
| **Stunner & Stun Combo** | Combo System | Simple inputs that trigger stun states, opening up realistic combo routes. | Accessible, high-damage follow-ups |

These mechanics ensure that players cannot rely solely on legacy strategies. The addition of Break & Rush means that defending the same blocking angle repeatedly can lead to a broken guard state that persists into subsequent rounds. Meanwhile, Flow Guard offers a stylish, active alternative to passive blocking, allowing skilled players to turn defense directly into a devastating counter-offensive.

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Relying too heavily on passive blocking will make you highly vulnerable to throws and the cumulative guard-breaking effects of the Break & Rush system. Mix up your defensive options regularly.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Step-by-Step Scenario Battle Progression Guide">

To successfully navigate the escalating challenges of the Vilasapara campaign, players should follow a structured training path. Because the game's single-player encounters are designed to prepare you for competitive play, building solid fundamentals early is essential.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Combat Triangle">
    Before diving into advanced mechanics, commit the strike-throw-guard triangle to memory. Practice recognizing when your opponent is playing passively so you can execute a throw, and use quick strikes to interrupt their throw attempts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Build Comfort with Core Controls">
    Spend time in practice modes getting comfortable with the standard Punch (P), Kick (K), and Guard (G) inputs. Practice basic combinations like P+K and PK to ensure your input timing is clean and consistent from both sides of the screen.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Separate Standing and Crouching Guard">
    Understand the strengths and limitations of your defensive options. Use Standing Guard to block high and mid-level attacks, but remember it loses to low attacks and throws. Transition to Crouching Guard to avoid high attacks and low strikes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Practice Turn-Taking and Punishes">
    Virtua Fighter combat is highly frame-conscious. When you successfully block an opponent's heavy strike, it is your "turn" to attack. Immediately follow up with a fast mid-level strike to punish their recovery frames before they can block.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Integrate Scenario-Specific Systems">
    Once your fundamentals are secure, begin incorporating Flow Guard and Stunner attacks into your playstyle. Use Stunner strikes to open up opponents, then execute a Stun Combo to maximize your damage output without needing overly complex inputs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Progression Tip">
Use the single-player Story Mode as a safe environment to test new characters and mechanics. The habits you build while exploring Vilasapara will carry over directly into online Battle Mode matches.
</Callout>
