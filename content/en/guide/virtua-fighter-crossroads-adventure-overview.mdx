<Section icon={Swords} title="Evolved Combat & Battle Systems">

At its core, the game preserves the classic close-range spacing and high-speed reads that defined the franchise's legacy [1]. The combat system relies on a strict **strike-throw-guard triangle** [1]. Strikes interrupt throw attempts, guarding blocks incoming strikes, and throws punish passive guarding [1]. However, RGG Studio has introduced several modern mechanics that add tactical depth, visual flair, and long-term strategic planning to every match [1].

| Combat System | Category | How It Works | Strategic Player Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Rules** | Battle Rule | Traditional round-based matches decided by health depletion or ring outs [1]. | Focuses on classic stage control, spacing, and round-by-round adjustments [1]. |
| **Uprising Rules** | Battle Rule | A separate, dynamic ruleset built around shifting match momentum [1]. | Encourages aggressive play and comebacks, altering standard match flow [1]. |
| **Break & Rush** | Offensive | Repeated strikes to specific body regions accumulate structural damage [1]. | Allows players to target weak points, carrying damage over between rounds [1]. |
| **Stunner** | Offensive | A specialized strike technique that leaves opponents highly vulnerable [1]. | Serves as a primary tool to initiate high-damage follow-up sequences [1]. |
| **Stun Combo** | Offensive | A streamlined combo system designed for fluid, realistic sequences [1]. | Lowers the execution barrier for beginners while maintaining technical depth [1]. |
| **Flow Guard** | Defensive | An active, stylish defensive maneuver emphasizing fluid movement [1]. | Provides a visual and functional counter to aggressive Break & Rush pressure [1]. |

To accommodate both competitive veterans and newcomers, the developers are offering two distinct rulesets in versus play, allowing players to customize their experience [1].

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Beginner Training Path & Progression">

Transitioning into high-level 3D fighting games can be intimidating, but following a structured training path ensures a smooth learning curve [1]. Before the game launches, players can build fundamental muscle memory using current franchise tools and carry those habits directly into the new systems [1].

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Combat Triangle">
    Begin by practicing the fundamental relationship between strikes, throws, and guards [1]. Spend time in training modes recognizing that strikes beat throws, guards stop strikes, and throws punish defensive blocking [1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Internalize Basic Notation">
    Get comfortable with standard inputs: Punch (P), Kick (K), and Guard (G) [1]. Practice simple combinations like PK (Punch then Kick) and P+K (simultaneous press) from both left and right facing positions [1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Differentiate High, Mid, and Low Guards">
    Learn when to use Standing Guard (blocks high/mid but loses to lows and throws) versus Crouching Guard (blocks lows and evades high throws, but loses to devastating mid-level strikes) [1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Practice Turn-Taking Discipline">
    Avoid button-mashing [1]. After successfully blocking an opponent's attack, recognize that it is your "turn" to strike back [1]. Start with fast, simple mid attacks to punish their recovery frames [1].
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Integrate New Crossroads Systems">
    Once your fundamentals are solid, layer on the new mechanics [1]. Practice initiating combos with a **Stunner** strike, executing **Stun Combos**, and using **Flow Guard** to actively redirect aggressive opponents [1].
  </Step>
</Steps>
