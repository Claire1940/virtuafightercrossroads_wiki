<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Evolution: Uprising vs Standard Rules">

Virtua Fighter Crossroads retains the classic "Punch, Kick, Guard" philosophy but introduces several layers of tactical depth. The most significant addition is the **Uprising Ruleset**, which serves as an alternative to the traditional Standard rules. While Standard rules focus on health depletion and ring-outs, Uprising is designed around dynamic momentum shifts that can change the flow of a match instantly.

| Mechanic | Category | Function | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Break & Rush** | **Offense** | Damage specific body regions | Status carries over between rounds |
| **Flow Guard** | **Defense** | Active, fluid defensive motion | Reverses momentum after a block |
| **Stunner** | **Strike** | Opens opponent to follow-ups | Leads directly into Stun Combos |
| **Stun Combo** | **Combo** | Simplified, fluid attack routes | Accessible damage for new players |
| **Uprising Rules** | **Rule Set** | Momentum-shifting battle logic | High-stakes, dramatic match flow |

The **Break & Rush** system is particularly noteworthy. By targeting specific areas of an opponent's body, players can inflict "Broken" status. Unlike previous games where every round reset the state of the fighters, these injuries persist, forcing players to adapt their strategy as the match progresses. This adds a layer of long-term planning to the high-speed 3D combat.

<Callout type="warning" title="Strategic Depth">
In Virtua Fighter Crossroads, passive guarding is more dangerous than ever. The introduction of Flow Guard and Break attacks means that players must be proactive in their defense rather than simply holding the guard button.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Beginner Training Path for Crossroads">

If you are new to the series, the transition to Virtua Fighter Crossroads might seem daunting. However, the game is being built with an "intuitive control" philosophy. To prepare for the 2027 launch, players should focus on the core triangle of Virtua Fighter combat: Strikes, Throws, and Guards.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Triangle">
    Understand the basic logic: Strikes beat Throws, Guards stop Strikes, and Throws punish Guards. This is the foundation of every high-level read.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Learn Notation">
    Virtua Fighter uses P (Punch), K (Kick), and G (Guard). Practice basic combinations like P+K or K+G to understand how the game handles multi-button inputs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Practice Movement">
    3D movement is key. Learn to sidestep effectively to avoid linear strikes and reposition yourself for ring-outs or wall combos.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Study the New Systems">
    Once Crossroads launches, immediately jump into the tutorial for **Flow Guard** and **Stunner** techniques, as these will be essential for the new meta.
  </Step>
</Steps>
