<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Combat Mechanics: Uprising and Break & Rush">

The combat in **virtua fighter crossroads akira** evolves the traditional "Punch, Kick, Guard" triangle. While the core logic remains—strikes beat throws, throws beat guards, and guards beat strikes—RGG Studio has introduced cinematic systems that add layers of strategy to every exchange.

The most significant addition is the **Uprising Rules**, a separate ruleset from the classic **Standard Rules**. Uprising focuses on momentum shifts and cinematic payoffs, making it the default for Story Mode encounters.

| System | Category | Mechanic Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Break & Rush** | Offensive | Target specific body parts to cause "Break" status. |
| **Flow Guard** | Defensive | A timed defensive move that transitions into a counter. |
| **Stunner** | Strike | A heavy blow that opens the opponent to Stun Combos. |
| **Stun Combo** | Combo | Fluid, realistic follow-ups that don't require juggles. |

<Callout type="success" title="System Mastery">
The **Break & Rush** system is persistent. Damage dealt to an opponent's arm or leg in Round 1 carries over to Round 2, potentially disabling certain moves later in the match.
</Callout>

### The New Defensive Meta
**Flow Guard** replaces the static blocking of the past with a more active system. By timing your guard, you can deflect incoming strikes with visual flair, mirroring the "action movie" presentation RGG Studio is known for. This works in tandem with the **Cinematic Hit Presentation**, where decisive blows trigger dramatic camera angles to emphasize the impact.
