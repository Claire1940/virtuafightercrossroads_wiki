<Section icon={Target} title="Advanced Combat Mechanics: Break, Rush, and Flow">

The combat in Virtua Fighter Crossroads preserves the "Strike-Throw-Guard" triangle but adds layers of cinematic realism. The most significant addition is the **Break & Rush** system, which allows players to target specific body regions to accumulate long-term damage that persists across rounds. This shifts the meta from simple health depletion to strategic "crippling" of an opponent's offensive tools.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate Stunner">
    Use the new Stunner strike technique to open the opponent's guard and create a window for follow-up attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Accumulate Region Damage">
    Focus attacks on arms or legs. Repeated hits to these regions build toward a "Break" state.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Break Attack">
    Once the threshold is met, land a Break Attack to inflict a permanent debuff for the remainder of the match.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Flow Guard Defense">
    Utilize Flow Guard to negate momentum and transition directly from a defensive posture into a counter-rush.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| System | Category | Core Mechanic | Player Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Rules** | **Classic** | Health / Ring-out victory | Traditional competitive 3D fighting logic. |
| **Uprising Rules** | **New** | Momentum-based scoring | Dynamic match flow with shifting advantages. |
| **Stun Combo** | **Offensive** | Fluid, realistic follow-ups | Easier access to damaging routes for beginners. |
| **Flow Guard** | **Defensive** | Active motion-based blocking | Stylish defense that interacts with Break states. |

<Callout type="success" title="Strategic Depth">
The Break & Rush system means that even if you lose a round, the damage you inflicted on an opponent's "kicking leg" carries over, making them slower and less lethal in the next round.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Beginner Progression and Training Path">

For players new to Virtua Fighter, Crossroads offers an intuitive entry point. Unlike other fighters that rely on complex "quarter-circle" inputs, Virtua Fighter focuses on timing, positioning, and the fundamental relationship between Punch (P), Kick (K), and Guard (G).

| Step | Focus | Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Step 1** | **The Triangle** | Understand that Strikes beat Throws, Throws beat Guard, and Guard beats Strikes. |
| **Step 2** | **Basic Controls** | Master P, K, and G combinations like P+K (Dual Strike) and P+G (Throw). |
| **Step 3** | **Movement** | Learn to sidestep and backdash to create whiff punish opportunities. |
| **Step 4** | **Stunner Usage** | Practice the Stunner to Stun Combo transition for reliable damage. |

<Callout type="tip" title="Starter Characters">
Cielo is the recommended starting character for beginners. His MMA style is rooted in boxing, providing a familiar and effective striking-focused toolkit for learning the game's pace.
</Callout>
