<Section icon={Shield} title="Gameplay Mechanics and Combat Systems">

The combat in **virtua fighter crossroads** maintains the series' traditional "Strike-Throw-Guard" triangle but introduces several new layers to modernize the 3D fighting experience. The two primary rulesets, **Standard** and **Uprising**, offer different ways to engage in combat.

| System | Category | How it Works |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Break & Rush** | Offensive | Damage builds in body regions; triggers a "Break" attack. |
| **Flow Guard** | Defensive | A fluid defensive move that counters Break & Rush attempts. |
| **Stunner** | Strike | A specialized attack that opens opponents to Stun Combos. |
| **Stun Combo** | Combo | A simplified but deep combo system for cinematic damage. |
| **Uprising Rules** | Battle Rule | A momentum-based ruleset separate from classic rounds. |

The "Break & Rush" system is particularly innovative, as damage to specific body parts (like arms or legs) carries over across rounds in a match. This forces players to think about long-term strategy rather than just immediate health depletion.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Beginner Guide: Mastering the Basics">

If you are preparing for the **virtua fighter crossroads switch 2** launch, mastering the fundamentals is essential. Virtua Fighter is unique because it doesn't rely on projectiles; it is a game of spacing and timing.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Learn the Buttons">
    Focus on the three core inputs: **Punch (P)**, **Kick (K)**, and **Guard (G)**. Most complex moves are combinations of these three.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Practice the Triangle">
    Spend time in Tutorial Mode learning how to throw an opponent who is blocking too much, and how to strike an opponent who is trying to throw you.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master Movement">
    3D movement is vital. Use side-stepping to avoid linear strikes and maintain control of the center of the ring to avoid a Ring Out.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Understand High/Mid/Low">
    Standing Guard blocks High and Mid. Crouching Guard blocks Low. Remember: Mid attacks are often the most dangerous as they hit crouching opponents.
  </Step>
</Steps>
