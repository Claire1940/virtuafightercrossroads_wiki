<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Systems and Battle Mechanics">

While the "Adventure" side is new, the "Fighting" side is built on the rock-solid foundation of the original series. The **virtua fighter crossroads trailer** and developer showcases have highlighted several new mechanics designed to make the game more dynamic without losing its technical depth.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Triangle">
    Understand the core logic: **Strikes** beat throws, **Guards** block strikes, and **Throws** punish passive guarding. This remains the heart of the 1v1 experience.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize Break & Rush">
    Target specific body regions to build damage. Once a region is "broken," the status carries over between rounds, rewarding long-term strategy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Engage Flow Guard">
    Use the new defensive system for fluid, cinematic evasions. Flow Guard shifts the momentum and opens up counter-attack windows.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Stun Combos">
    Land a **Stunner** strike to open the opponent. Follow up with intuitive **Stun Combos** that prioritize realism and visual impact over complex inputs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Mechanic | Category | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Rules** | Battle Rule | Classic health depletion and ring-out victory |
| **Uprising Rules** | Battle Rule | New momentum-shifting scoring and match flow |
| **Stunner** | Strike Tech | A specialized attack that initiates combo routes |
| **Cinematic Hits** | Presentation | Action-movie camera angles for decisive strikes |

The addition of **Uprising Rules** is particularly interesting for competitive players. It offers a separate way to play that emphasizes aggressive momentum and dramatic turnarounds, contrasting with the traditional round-based health management of **Standard Rules**.

<Callout type="info" title="Control Philosophy">
RGG Studio is rebuilding the controls to be "intuitive." This means the basic Punch, Kick, and Guard (P, K, G) inputs remain, but the timing for advanced combos and defensive maneuvers is being streamlined for a modern audience.
</Callout>
