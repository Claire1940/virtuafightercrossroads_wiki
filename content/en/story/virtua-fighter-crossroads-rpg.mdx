<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Training Path">

For players new to the franchise or those transitioning from RGG's action games, mastering the **virtua fighter crossroads rpg** combat requires a shift in mindset. Unlike 2D fighters with fireballs, this is a game of inches, frame advantage, and close-range reads.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Triangle">
    Understand the core logic: Strikes beat Throws, Guards beat Strikes, and Throws beat Guards. Every exchange in the game revolves around this fundamental RPS (Rock-Paper-Scissors) relationship.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Input Comfort">
    Practice the simple P (Punch), K (Kick), and G (Guard) layout. Learn basic notations like P+K or K+G, which are essential for executing the new Stunner and Break attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Defensive Discipline">
    Differentiate between Standing Guard (blocks high/mid) and Crouching Guard (blocks low/avoids throws). Use the new Flow Guard to create openings against aggressive opponents.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Story to Versus Transition">
    Use the single-player Story Mode to learn character-specific habits. The "Fighting Adventure" structure is designed to teach you mechanics through narrative encounters before you enter the competitive Battle Mode.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Starter Characters">
If you are struggling with the technicality, start with **Cielo**. His striking-focused MMA style is designed to be the most accessible entry point for modern players, emphasizing direct pressure and boxing-root movements.
</Callout>

</Section>
