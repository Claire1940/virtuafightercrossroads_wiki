<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Gameplay Systems">

Combat in **Virtua Fighter Crossroads** evolves the series' signature 3D movement and close-range "Strike-Throw-Guard" triangle. RGG Studio has introduced several new systems designed to make matches more dynamic and visually impactful while maintaining the high skill ceiling the franchise is known for.

The most significant addition is the **Break & Rush** system. This mechanic allows players to target specific body regions on an opponent. Consistent pressure on the arms, legs, or torso accumulates damage that eventually triggers a "Broken" state. This status persists across rounds, forcing players to adapt their strategy as their character's mobility or offensive power diminishes.

| System | Category | Primary Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Rules** | **Rule Set** | Traditional health depletion and ring outs |
| **Uprising Rules** | **Rule Set** | Momentum-based scoring and dynamic flow |
| **Break & Rush** | **Offense** | Persistent limb and body part damage |
| **Flow Guard** | **Defense** | Active defensive movement with visual flair |
| **Stunner** | **Technique** | New combo starters for high-damage routes |
| **Stun Combo** | **Combo** | Simplified, fluid follow-ups after a Stunner |

**Flow Guard** serves as a cinematic defensive tool, allowing characters to parry or evade attacks with fluid animations that transition directly into counter-offensive opportunities. This system works in tandem with the **Stunner** technique, which opens up new combo paths without requiring the complex frame-perfect execution of older titles, making the game more accessible to newcomers.

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategy Note">
Use the **Break & Rush** system to disable opponents who rely on specific moves. If a character has a powerful kick, targeting their legs can eventually reduce the speed and damage of those specific attacks.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Beginner Training and Fundamentals">

For players new to the series, **Virtua Fighter Crossroads** can be intimidating due to its lack of projectiles and focus on realistic physics. However, the game is built on a simple foundation that anyone can master with practice. The core of every fight is the interaction between three basic actions: **Punch (P)**, **Kick (K)**, and **Guard (G)**.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Triangle">
    Understand that Strikes (P/K) beat Throws, Throws beat Guarding, and Guarding beats Strikes. This fundamental logic dictates the flow of every match.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Learn Guard Heights">
    Standing Guard protects against High and Mid attacks but loses to Lows and Throws. Crouching Guard avoids Highs and blocks Lows but is vulnerable to Mids.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Practice Turn-Taking">
    After blocking an opponent's attack, you usually have a "frame advantage." This is your "turn" to strike back. Don't stay defensive forever.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Explore Stunner Routes">
    In the Crossroads training mode, focus on landing **Stunner** attacks. These are the easiest way for beginners to access damaging **Stun Combos**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Training in **Virtua Fighter Crossroads** is designed to be intuitive. The Story Mode acts as a massive tutorial, introducing these concepts through low-stakes encounters before the player enters the highly competitive **Battle Mode**.

<Callout type="info" title="Starter Characters">
Cielo is highly recommended for beginners. His MMA style is rooted in boxing, offering straightforward striking patterns and easy-to-understand defensive options that align well with the new **Flow Guard** system.
</Callout>
