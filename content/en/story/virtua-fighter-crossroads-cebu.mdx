<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Training Guide for Cebuano Beginners">

Virtua Fighter has a reputation for being a highly technical, "eye-candy" 3D fighter, but producer Riichiro Yamada has emphasized that Virtua Fighter Crossroads is designed to be highly accessible. You do not need to memorize complex 50-input combos to be competitive. The game revolves around quick close-range reads and fundamental decision-making.

For beginners in the Cebu scene, here is a structured step-by-step training roadmap to prepare your muscle memory before the game launches in 2027.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the Strike-Throw-Guard Triangle">
    Understand that Virtua Fighter is a high-speed game of rock-paper-scissors. Striking beats throw attempts, guarding blocks incoming strikes, and throwing punishes passive blocking. Never stay frozen in one option; alternate your defensive and offensive habits constantly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Build Comfort with Basic Inputs">
    Virtua Fighter uses a simple three-button layout: Punch (P), Kick (K), and Guard (G). Practice simple notations like PK (Punch then Kick) and P+K (pressing both simultaneously). Building consistent execution of these basic inputs is more important than memorizing long combo routes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Learn Active Spacing and Movement">
    Because there are no fireballs or long-range projectiles, Virtua Fighter is entirely played at close-to-mid range. Practice lateral movement, sidestepping to evade linear strikes, and maintaining a distance where you can punish your opponent's missed attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Differentiate High, Mid, and Low Defense">
    Standing Guard protects you from high and mid attacks but leaves you vulnerable to low sweeps and throws. Crouching Guard evades high attacks and blocks lows but loses to powerful mid-level strikes. Learn to recognize your opponent's attack animations to adjust your guard.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Transition Your Skills into Story Mode">
    When Virtua Fighter Crossroads launches, use the single-player Story Mode in Vilasapara as your primary training ground. The campaign is designed to naturally teach you character-specific techniques, multi-opponent brawling, and boss fight strategies that directly carry over to versus play.
  </Step>
</Steps>
